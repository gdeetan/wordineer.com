5-letter words starting with A

The letter A starts more five-letter words than most other letters. From common everyday words like agree, alive, and anger to less familiar terms like argot, augur, and amity, the A list covers a wide range of difficulty levels, word types, and use cases. Whether you're looking for a Wordle opener, a Scrabble play, a vocabulary exercise, or a writing prompt, this list is a practical starting point.

A is a vowel, which gives A words a structural advantage in word games — they tend to pair well with other common letters and leave space for high-frequency consonants like R, S, L, T, and N. Many of the most versatile five-letter A words combine two of these consonants alongside the A, making them efficient guesses that test large portions of the alphabet in a single play.

5-letter A words for Wordle

Wordle players have strong reasons to study the A section of any five-letter word list. The letter A is one of the most common vowels in five-letter Wordle answers, appearing in roughly 40% of all solutions. A strong Wordle strategy involves testing A in your first or second guess — and knowing which five-letter A words cover the most new consonants helps you gather information faster.

Best A words to open with

The goal of an opening guess is to test as many common letters as possible. Five-letter A words that cover a spread of high-frequency consonants without repeating letters are the most valuable openers.

AriseAdoreAlertAlterAmendAvertAvoid

Arise (A, R, I, S, E) and adore (A, D, O, R, E) both test five different high-frequency letters in one guess. Alert covers A, L, E, R, T — four of the six most common letters in Wordle answers. These words don't waste letters on repeats, so every tile you reveal carries new information.

A words to know for a stuck Wordle

When you've confirmed A is in the word but can't figure out the rest, the medium and hard sections of this list become valuable. Words like askew, angst, abide, acute, and aglow cover less common letter combinations that are easy to miss under pressure. 5-letter A words for Scrabble and word games

In Scrabble, five-letter A words are valuable when you have an A tile you need to build around. A is a 1-point tile, so the value comes from the other letters in the word — but placing a five-letter word that uses an A efficiently can open up or block key board positions.

High-value A words worth memorising for Scrabble include abuzz (Z is worth 10 points), ascot, axiom (X is worth 8 points), and azure (Z again). Words with uncommon letters — Q, X, Z, J — are worth memorising specifically because they're hard to play and having a short, valid word for them is a consistent competitive advantage.

For two-player word games like Words With Friends, the scoring system differs slightly from Scrabble, but the principle is the same: knowing obscure short words with high-value letters is more useful than knowing long common ones.

5-letter A words for vocabulary building

If you're using this list for vocabulary study rather than word games, the Medium and Hard difficulty tiers are where the learning happens. Easy words — agree, alive, alter — are already in your active vocabulary. The interesting work starts at medium difficulty.

Medium-difficulty A words worth knowing

Words like abode (a place of residence), adage (a traditional saying), aloft (high in the air), amiss (slightly wrong), and askew (crooked or lopsided) are the kind of words that appear in well-written prose but rarely in everyday conversation. Knowing them on sight and being able to use them precisely is a real vocabulary gain.

Hard A words that reward study

The hard tier covers words that are uncommon, specialised, or archaic — but each one is genuinely useful in the right context. Aegis means protection or sponsorship (as in "under the aegis of"). Augur means to be a sign of something to come (as in "this augurs well"). Argot means the jargon of a specific group. Amity means peaceful friendship. Ardor means intense enthusiasm. These words carry specific meanings that simpler synonyms don't fully capture — and they're the words that make writing precise rather than approximate.

Frequently asked questions

How many 5-letter words start with A?

Standard English dictionaries contain between 400 and 800 five-letter words beginning with A, depending on the source. Common word game dictionaries used for Wordle and Scrabble typically include 400–600. This list covers the most useful ones, tagged by type and difficulty.

What are the best 5-letter words starting with A for Wordle?

Strong opening words starting with A include arise, adore, alert, audio, and alike — they cover high-frequency letters like R, S, L, T, E, I, O, and U. Filter this list to Easy difficulty to see the words most likely to appear as Wordle answers.

Are these words valid for Scrabble?

Most standard words on this list are valid in Scrabble, but the official Scrabble word list (TWL for North America, SOWPODS for international play) is the authoritative source. Rare or archaic words marked as Hard difficulty may or may not be accepted depending on which ruleset you're using.

What does the difficulty rating mean?

Easy words are common everyday vocabulary most adult speakers know. Medium words are less frequent but widely understood. Hard words are uncommon, specialised, or archaic — useful for advanced vocabulary study or competitive Scrabble. Ratings are based on word frequency in standard English usage.

What 5-letter words starting with A have no repeated letters?

Five-letter A words with no repeated letters include arise, adore, alert, agile, amend, avert, avoid, and angle. These are particularly useful in Wordle because each letter provides new information about the puzzle — no letter is "wasted" on a repeat.
