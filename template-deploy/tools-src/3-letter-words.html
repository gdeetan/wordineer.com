About three-letter words

Three-letter words do a surprising amount of work in English. They are the first words most children learn to read and write, and they are some of the most strategically valuable plays in competitive word games. This page brings the full set together — organized by starting letter — so you can filter, study, or copy exactly what you need.

For word games and Scrabble

Knowing the full inventory of three-letter words for each letter is one of the most effective ways to improve at Scrabble, Words With Friends, and similar games. Short words slot into tight spaces, allow parallel plays, and help you unload difficult tiles. Letters like Q, X, and Z have very few valid three-letter words, which makes knowing each one precisely valuable — you cannot guess your way to qua, xis, or zax.

For phonics and early literacy

Three-letter words are the backbone of early reading instruction. CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) words like cat, dog, hat, and pen are the first decodable patterns most children encounter. The Easy filter on this page isolates those high-frequency forms, making it useful for teachers building spelling lists, parents supporting home reading, and anyone working with early readers.

For vocabulary study

Short words can carry precise and unusual meanings. Moving past the easy tier reveals entries worth learning: adz (a woodworking blade), froe — wait, that is four letters. But fen is a marsh, mho is a unit of conductance, and phi is a Greek letter with mathematical significance. The Hard filter surfaces these compact but exact words.

Frequently asked questions

How many 3-letter words are there in English?

Major Scrabble dictionaries list roughly 1,000 to 1,300 valid three-letter words in English, depending on the word list used. This tool covers curated sets for each letter, ranging from everyday vocabulary to rarer archaic and dialect forms — all with definitions so you can judge what is worth learning.

Why are 3-letter words so important in Scrabble?

Three-letter words are the backbone of board word games. They fit into narrow gaps on the board, enable parallel plays alongside existing words, and let you unload difficult tiles without giving up tempo. Players who know the full three-letter inventory for every letter have a measurable edge over those who only know the common ones.

Why do some letters have fewer words than others?

English genuinely has fewer three-letter words starting with X, Q, Y, and Z than it does for common letters like S, T, P, or C. This reflects the frequency of those letters in the language, not a gap in the data. The counts on this page reflect real vocabulary — we do not pad with invented or questionable entries.

What does the difficulty rating mean?

Easy words are common everyday vocabulary that most readers encounter regularly. Medium words are less frequent but still broadly understandable. Hard words are rarer, more technical, regional, borrowed, or archaic forms that are most useful for advanced vocabulary work and competitive word games.
