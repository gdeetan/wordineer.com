3-letter words starting with V

V is one of the tighter starting-letter groups in English, which means a good V list needs to be curated rather than padded. This page brings together 99 three-letter entries that actually do useful work: familiar everyday words like van, vat, vet, and vow; game-helpful oddities such as vig, vis, voe, vox, and vug; and a modern layer of widely encountered short forms such as VAT, VFX, VHF, VPN, and VUI.

That mixed structure matters because most users do not come to a V page for the same reason. Some need short playable forms for Scrabble. Some need a compact browseable glossary. Others just want a cleaner, better-defined list than a random dictionary scrape. The filters help you move between those jobs quickly without changing page style or layout.

Common 3-letter V words

The everyday core is small but genuinely useful. Words like van, vat, vet, via, and vow are common enough to matter in ordinary reading and classroom vocabulary. Veg and vac add familiar informal usage, while vee is worth knowing because it appears in descriptions of shapes, formations, and lettering.

Even among the easy entries, V has more range than it first appears. Vet can name a profession or mean to examine something carefully. Via works as a routing word in travel, logistics, writing, and email. Vow belongs equally to religion, law, ceremony, and personal promise. That variety is why short V forms are worth studying rather than dismissing as filler.

3-letter V words for Scrabble and word games

The strict lowercase V set is small enough that memorising most of it is realistic, and that is exactly what gives it value in game play. Because V is a 4-point tile, even a short legal word can carry more weight than its length suggests. Entries like var, vas, vig, vim, vis, vox, and vug are the kind of compact plays that rescue awkward racks or open a tight board.

Short V plays worth memorising

If you only learn one slice of the page for game use, focus on the uncommon lowercase forms rather than the obvious everyday words. Vig means a bookmaker's commission, vis is a learned word for force, voe is a Shetland inlet, and vug is a tiny rock cavity often lined with crystals. These are easy to forget because they live outside ordinary conversation, but that is exactly why they pay off under pressure.

No-repeat V words for clue-based games

When you want every letter to test different information, no-repeat forms help. van, var, vas, vat, vex, via, vie, vim, vin, vox, and vug all avoid repeated letters. That makes them more efficient in Wordle-style clue systems and other elimination games.

3-letter V words for vocabulary building

The technical layer is where the V page becomes more than a narrow word-game reference. Three-letter V forms show up constantly in medicine, finance, aviation, media, networking, and software. Readers regularly encounter VAT, VCR, VFX, VHF, VOD, VPN, VPS, VUI, VWD, and VWF in real documents, product pages, or specialist conversations.
