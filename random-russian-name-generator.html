    What is a random Russian name generator?

    A random Russian name generator creates Russian-style given names, surnames, and full names for fictional characters, games, writing prompts, and naming research. This tool can show a name in Latin transliteration, Cyrillic, or both, and it can include meaning notes and familiar short forms such as Sasha, Dima, Masha, or Katya.

    Russian names often carry more structure than a simple first-name and last-name pair. A formal full name commonly includes a given name, a patronymic, and a surname. The generator uses that structure when you choose full-name or formal-order output.

    How it works

    Choose how many names you want, then pick filters such as gender, name type, script, style, nickname display, and starting letter. When you change a filter, the list updates automatically.

    For full names, the tool chooses a given name, selects a compatible surname form, and creates a patronymic from a male given name. Male forms use endings like -ovich or -evich, while female forms often use -ovna or -evna. Many Russian surnames also change by gender, so Ivanov becomes Ivanova for a female name.

    Understanding Russian names

    A common formal pattern is given name + patronymic + surname. For example, a male full name might use Ivan Petrovich Sokolov, while a female full name might use Maria Petrovna Sokolova. First name + patronymic is also a respectful form of address in many formal or professional settings.

    Nicknames and short forms are common in everyday speech. Alexander may become Sasha, Dmitri may become Dima, Natalia may become Natasha, and Ekaterina may become Katya. Turn on the nickname filter when you want those familiar forms shown under the result.

    Russian name generator FAQ

    Are the names realistic?
    They are built from recognizable Russian given names, patronymics, and gendered surname forms. They are best for fiction, games, and brainstorming, not official identity research.
    Why do surnames change by gender?
    Many Russian surnames have masculine and feminine forms. A male surname such as Ivanov, Petrov, or Sokolov commonly becomes Ivanova, Petrova, or Sokolova for a female name.
    What is a patronymic?
    A patronymic is a middle-name-like form based on the father's given name. For example, a patronymic from Ivan can be Ivanovich for a man or Ivanovna for a woman.
    Can I copy or save names?
    Yes. Use Copy all for the current list, or click the heart beside individual names to collect a saved list.
