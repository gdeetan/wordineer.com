How Powerball numbers are drawn

Powerball draws 5 white balls from a pool of 69 numbered balls (1–69), then 1 red Powerball from a separate pool of 26 balls (1–26). The white balls must all be different — no repeats — but the Powerball is drawn from its own independent pool, so it can match one of the white balls. To win the jackpot, you must match all 5 white balls in any order plus the Powerball. This generator replicates that exact format: 5 unique numbers from 1–69 chosen without replacement, plus 1 independent number from 1–26.

Does a random number generator improve your odds?

No. Every combination of 5 white balls + 1 Powerball is equally likely. Whether you pick your own "lucky" numbers or use a random generator, the probability of winning the jackpot is exactly 1 in 292,201,338. Research on historical lottery draws shows no statistically significant pattern in winning numbers — the draws are designed to be completely random. The only advantage of using a generator is avoiding unconscious biases like picking birthdays (capped at 31) or repeating previous numbers.

What are the odds of winning Powerball?
The jackpot odds are 1 in 292,201,338 — you must match all 5 white balls plus the Powerball. Matching just the Powerball (no white balls) has odds of 1 in 38.32, which wins a $4 prize. There are 9 total prize tiers, ranging from matching only the Powerball up to the jackpot.
