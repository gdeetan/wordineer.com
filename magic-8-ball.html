How does the Magic 8 Ball work?

The original Magic 8 Ball toy, invented in 1950, contains a 20-sided icosahedral die floating in dark blue liquid. Each face is printed with one of 20 responses — 10 positive, 5 neutral, and 5 negative. When you shake the ball, the die floats to the top window and reveals an answer. This digital version replicates the same 20 classic responses with equal probability weighting: each response has exactly a 5% chance of appearing on any given shake.

The 20 classic responses

Positive (50%): It is certain · It is decidedly so · Without a doubt · Yes definitely · You may rely on it · As I see it, yes · Most likely · Outlook good · Yes · Signs point to yes

Neutral (25%): Reply hazy, try again · Ask again later · Better not tell you now · Cannot predict now · Concentrate and ask again

Negative (25%): Don't count on it · My reply is no · My sources say no · Outlook not so good · Very doubtful

Tips for asking the Magic 8 Ball

The Magic 8 Ball is designed for yes/no questions. Think of your question clearly before shaking — the toy tradition is to hold it in your hands, concentrate on your question, then turn it over to reveal the answer. Questions like "Will I get the job?" or "Should I go to the party?" work well. Open-ended questions like "What should I do?" don't have meaningful answers from the 20 responses.

Neutral (5): Reply hazy try again, Ask again later, Better not tell you now, Cannot predict now, Concentrate and ask again. Is the Magic 8 Ball random?
Yes. This digital Magic 8 Ball uses JavaScript's Math.random() with equal weighting across all 20 responses. Each answer has exactly a 5% chance of appearing on any given shake — the same probability distribution as a physical 20-sided die floating in a toy ball.
What questions can I ask the Magic 8 Ball?
Yes/no questions work best. Examples: "Will I get the job?", "Should I go to the party?", "Is this a good idea?", "Will it rain tomorrow?". Open-ended questions like "What should I do?" don't have meaningful answers from the 20 fixed responses — the Magic 8 Ball is designed to give a directional yes, no, or neutral answer to a specific question.
How does the Magic 8 Ball work?
The original Magic 8 Ball toy was invented in 1950 by Mattel. It contains a 20-sided icosahedral die floating in dark blue liquid inside a black plastic ball shaped like a billiard 8 ball. Each face of the die is printed with one of 20 responses. When you shake the ball and turn it over, the die floats to the top window and reveals an answer. This digital version replicates the same 20 classic responses using Math.random() with equal probability weighting.
