How to unscramble letters into words

Type your scrambled letters into the input box — up to 15 at once — and press Enter or click Find Words. The tool searches through tens of thousands of English words and returns every valid word that can be formed from your letters, grouped by word length from longest to shortest. The longest words appear at the top because they're typically the most valuable in word games.

Use ? for a blank tile or wildcard. A single ? can stand in for any letter, so entering aeirnt? will surface words that need one extra letter beyond what you have. This makes the tool practical for real Scrabble and Words With Friends rack situations where blank tiles are common.

Filters: starts with, ends with, must include

The Starts with, Ends with, and Must include filters are especially useful for crossword solving. If you know a word ends in -ing and starts with r, set both filters and cut your results to just the relevant words. The Must include filter ensures every result contains a specific letter or short sequence — handy when you need a word with a high-value letter like Q or Z.

The word length dropdown lets you narrow results to a specific length. The "7+ letters" option shows only longer words, which is ideal when you need a high-scoring Scrabble play or want to fill a long crossword slot.

Choosing the right dictionary

The dictionary selector lets you switch between three word lists. US English (NWL) is used in North American tournament Scrabble and is the strictest list. UK English (SOWPODS) is used in international Scrabble and includes more words. Common Words (ENABLE) is a broader everyday list that works well for casual games and Words With Friends. When in doubt, ENABLE gives the most results.

Scrabble points

Toggle on Show Scrabble points to see the tile value of every result — useful when you want the highest-scoring play, not just the longest word. A short word with a Q or Z can outscore a much longer word made of low-value tiles. Points are calculated using standard North American Scrabble tile values.

Frequently asked questions

How do I unscramble letters into words?
Type your letters into the input box and press Enter or click Find Words. The tool searches through tens of thousands of English words and returns every valid word that can be formed from your letters, grouped by length from longest to shortest. You can enter up to 15 letters and use ? as a wildcard for any blank tile.
Can I use this to solve Scrabble or Words With Friends?
Yes. Enter the letters on your rack — up to 15 at once. Add ? for each blank tile you have. The tool finds every valid word that can be formed from your letters and sorts results from longest to shortest. Toggle on Show Scrabble points to see tile values. Use Starts with and Ends with if you need to fit the word into a specific board position. You can switch between US English (NWL), UK English (SOWPODS), and the ENABLE word list to match the rules of your game.
What does the ? wildcard do?
A ? acts as a blank tile — it can stand in for any letter of the alphabet. So entering aeirnt? will find all words that can be made from those six letters plus one wildcard. Each ? counts as one character toward the 15-letter limit. Blank-tile support makes the tool practical for real Scrabble situations where blanks are common.
Which dictionary does the unscrambler use?
You can choose from three dictionaries using the dropdown in the options panel: US English (NWL), used in North American tournament Scrabble; UK English (SOWPODS), used in international Scrabble; and the ENABLE common-word list, a good middle ground for casual games and Words With Friends.
How many letters can I unscramble at once?
Up to 15 letters at once. You can also mix in ? wildcard characters — each ? counts as one letter slot but can stand in for any letter. So entering 10 regular letters plus 2 wildcards (12 characters total) will find words up to 12 letters long. Results are grouped by length, longest first, so the most useful words surface at the top.
