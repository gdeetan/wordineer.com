What makes a great dad joke?

A dad joke lives or dies by its pun. The best ones use a single word that sounds like something else — a homophone trick so obvious that you groan out loud. That groan is the whole point. Unlike a stand-up punchline, a dad joke doesn't try to surprise you; it telegraphs exactly where it's going and makes you cringe anyway.

The format usually comes in two flavors: the question-and-answer ("Why did the bicycle fall over? It was two-tired.") and the one-liner that sneaks up on you mid-sentence. Both follow the same rule: maximum wordplay, minimum setup.

Frequently asked questions

What makes something a dad joke?
A dad joke is a short, intentionally corny pun or wordplay gag. The humor is in the groan — it's obvious, it telegraphs the punchline, and it makes people roll their eyes. The worse the pun, the better the dad joke.
Are these jokes family-safe?
Yes — every joke is clean and appropriate for all ages, including kids. No adult humor, no edgy content.
Can I filter jokes by topic?
Yes. Use the topic filter to narrow jokes to food, animals, work, science, sports, seasonal occasions, or general topics.
Will jokes repeat?
Jokes won't repeat within a session until the entire filtered pool is exhausted. Then the pool resets and starts again.
What is the groan meter?
The groan meter lets you rate how painful each joke is — from 😐 (barely a groan) to 💀 (maximum dad energy). Your ratings are saved locally for the session.
