What is Word of the Day?

Word of the Day is a simple vocabulary habit: learn one useful word, understand what it means, then use it in your own writing or conversation. This tool keeps the routine short so you can build vocabulary without feeling like you are studying a full lesson.

How to use the tool

Start with today's word at the top of the page. Read the short definition, then read the plain-English explanation and example sentence. After that, write one sentence of your own using the word. If the word feels useful, save it and copy your saved list for later review.

Use the Surprise me button when you want extra practice. It gives you another vocabulary word without changing the official daily word.

Why this helps vocabulary stick

A word is easier to remember when you see it in context and use it right away. That is why this tool includes an example, a memory hook, and a quick quiz. The goal is not just to recognize the word once, but to make it usable.

For students

Use the daily word as a warm-up before homework or test prep. Try writing one original sentence, then check whether your sentence matches the meaning.

For writers

Save words that fit your style, topic, or tone. When you need a stronger word later, copy your saved list and try one in a draft.

For teachers

Use the page as a quick bell-ringer activity: read the word, ask students to guess the meaning from the example, then have everyone write a sentence.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Word of the Day tool?
It is a daily vocabulary tool that shows one word with its definition, example sentence, memory hook, and quick practice prompt.
How do I use today's word?
Read the meaning, study the example, then write one sentence of your own using the word. That small act of use helps the word stick.
Can I save words?
Yes. Click Save word to keep a word in your saved list during your visit. You can copy the full saved list when you are done.
Does the word change every day?
Yes. The main word is selected by date, so it stays stable throughout the day and changes the next day.
Is this tool free?
Yes. It is free, instant, and does not require an account.
Where do the words come from?
The daily entries are curated for vocabulary learning. For extra practice, the tool can also draw from Wordineer's existing word dataset.
