    What makes a word work in Pictionary?

    Not every noun is drawable. The best Pictionary words have a clear visual form — something the drawer can put on paper without writing a single letter. "Elephant" works. "Democracy" is a nightmare. That's why difficulty level matters more than category: hard words aren't just obscure, they're harder to represent visually.

    Use Easy for kids, first-timers, or when you want fast rounds with high energy. Medium is the sweet spot for most adult game nights — words are recognizable but require some creativity to draw. Hard is for groups that have played together a lot and want a challenge that produces genuinely funny misses.

    How to run a great game night round

    Generate your word list before the round starts and use the Hide button so words aren't visible on screen. Show only the drawer their word (tap or click a card to reveal it). Start the 60-second timer when the first mark hits the board. If the team guesses correctly before time runs out, the drawer earns the point — not the guesser. Rotate drawers every round.

    The "Reset repeats" button clears the session memory if you want to start the pool fresh — useful if you're playing multiple games in a row and want the same words back in rotation.

    Best categories for different groups

    Kids and families: Easy + Animals or Easy + Classic. Short, concrete words with obvious shapes. Avoid actions — they're hard for younger players to draw clearly.

    Office parties and casual groups: Medium + All Categories. Mix keeps it interesting without anyone feeling stuck. Add Funny for bigger reactions and more laughs.

    Competitive groups: Hard + Classic or Hard + Actions. Abstract concepts like "Optical illusion" or "Gravity" are genuinely difficult and produce memorable rounds.

    Christmas Pictionary

    Select the Christmas category for holiday parties, classroom games, and family gatherings. The word list leans toward imagery that's immediately seasonal — stockings, snowmen, gingerbread houses — so even players who aren't great at drawing can get a guess across quickly. Combine Medium difficulty with Christmas for the best balance between easy guesses and surprising moments.

    Printing word cards

    Click "Print cards" to generate a clean, ad-free print layout — two columns of word cards formatted for standard paper. This works well for larger groups where you want to hand physical cards to each drawer instead of passing a phone around.

    Pictionary FAQs

    What's the difference between Easy, Medium, and Hard?
    Easy words are concrete objects and simple actions — things anyone can sketch in a few lines (Cat, Pizza, Jumping). Medium words require more imagination from the drawer — recognizable but not instantly obvious (Trampoline, Sushi, Photobomb). Hard words are abstract or obscure — concepts with no single clear visual form (Nostalgia, Democracy, Spelunking). Start with Easy if your group includes kids or first-timers.
    Can I use this for kids?
    Yes. Easy + Animals or Easy + Classic gives you short, concrete words that are quick to draw and guess. Avoid the Actions category with younger players — drawing a verb is much harder than drawing a noun, and it tends to frustrate kids who are still developing their drawing skills.
    Does the generator repeat words?
    It avoids repeats for the duration of your session — once a word has appeared, it won't come up again until you click "Reset repeats." This is useful for long games where you're cycling through many rounds. If you change difficulty or category, the repeat history resets automatically so the pool stays full.
    Can I use this for Christmas Pictionary?
    Yes — choose the Christmas category. Easy Christmas words (Snowman, Candy cane, Santa) work well for classroom parties and family games with mixed ages. Medium adds trickier seasonal words like Gingerbread house, Sleigh, and Snowball fight. Hard includes things like Nutcracker, Frankincense, and Nativity scene — better for groups who want a real challenge.
    Can I print the word cards?
    Yes — click "Print cards" and the page switches to a clean, ad-free layout with two columns of word cards sized for standard paper. This is handy for larger groups where passing a phone around gets awkward, or for classroom activities where each student draws from a stack of physical cards.
