How the lottery number generator works

The generator uses the Fisher-Yates shuffle algorithm to pick 6 unique numbers from a pool of 1 to 49. The algorithm is the same technique used by physical lottery ball machines — every number in the range has an equal probability of appearing in any position. The balls are then sorted in ascending order and displayed with color-coded bands so you can read them at a glance.

Why use random numbers for the lottery?

Any combination has an equal probability of winning. However, choosing your own numbers based on birthdays or popular sequences (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) means you're more likely to share the jackpot if those numbers win. Studies of real lottery draws show that truly random picks are slightly better in expected value because they reduce the probability of splitting a prize with other players who chose the same "lucky" numbers.

The 6/49 format explained

The 6/49 format — pick 6 numbers from 1 to 49 — is one of the world's most common lottery structures. It's used by the UK National Lottery, Canada Lotto 6/49, and many other major draws. The odds of matching all 6 are 1 in 13,983,816. This tool matches that exact format so your generated numbers are ready to play directly.

Generating sets for an office pool

Use the Sets field to generate up to 10 lines at once. Each set is an independent draw — the same number can appear across different sets, just not within a single set. Use "Copy all sets" to get every line formatted as a plain-text list, ready to paste into a spreadsheet or group chat.

Frequently asked questions

How are lottery numbers generated?
This tool uses the Fisher-Yates shuffle algorithm to randomly pick 6 unique numbers from 1 to 49. Every number has an equal probability of appearing, matching the randomness of a physical lottery draw.
Can I generate multiple sets at once?
Yes. Set the Sets field to any number from 1–10 to generate multiple lines simultaneously. Each set is independently drawn — no duplicates within a set, but the same number can appear across sets.
Are randomly generated lottery numbers as good as picking my own?
Yes — every combination has an equal probability of winning. Random picks may actually be slightly better in practice because popular "lucky" numbers lead to shared jackpots when they win. Random numbers reduce the chance of splitting a prize.
What is the 1–49 lottery format?
The 6/49 format (pick 6 from 49) is used by the UK National Lottery, Canada Lotto 6/49, and many other draws worldwide. You pick 6 numbers and win prizes based on how many match the drawn numbers.
